A Traveler Who Cannot Sit Still
Poetry Pairing · About 15 Minutes
📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
Some people cannot stop wondering what lies past the edge of the map.
Alfred, Lord Tennyson published “Ulysses” in 1842. The speaker is the aging hero of Homer's Odyssey, long since returned home from his travels and now ruling a quiet kingdom. He should be content. Instead, he cannot stop thinking about the unknown, and he decides to set out again in old age rather than spend his last years doing nothing new. This is not one of this week's five required lessons; it is a short, optional page. It pairs naturally with Ransom's story, since Out of the Silent Planet also begins with someone unwillingly, then willingly, carried toward the unknown.
🎯 BY THE END OF THIS PAGE YOU WILL BE ABLE TO…
1
I will be able to describe what the speaker wants and why staying home no longer satisfies him.
2
I will be able to connect the poem's hunger for the unknown to the novel you are about to read.
📖 THE POEM
⚡ QUICK CHECK-IN
Have you ever finished something you worked hard for, then felt restless instead of satisfied? (No wrong answer.)

Read the poem once through, slowly. It is written as a single speech: Ulysses talking to himself about the life he has lived and the life he still wants.

ULYSSES (SELECTED LINES) · ALFRED, LORD TENNYSON (1842)
It little profits that an idle king,
By this still hearth, among these barren crags,
Match'd with an aged wife, I mete and dole
Unequal laws unto a savage race,
That hoard, and sleep, and feed, and know not me.
I cannot rest from travel: I will drink
Life to the lees…
…I am a part of all that I have met;
Yet all experience is an arch wherethro'
Gleams that untravell'd world whose margin fades
For ever and for ever when I move.
How dull it is to pause, to make an end,
To rust unburnish'd, not to shine in use!
…Come, my friends,
'Tis not too late to seek a newer world.
…for my purpose holds
To sail beyond the sunset, and the baths
Of all the western stars, until I die.
…We are not now that strength which in old days
Moved earth and heaven; that which we are, we are;
One equal temper of heroic hearts,
Made weak by time and fate, but strong in will
To strive, to seek, to find, and not to yield.
This poem is in the public domain. The lines above are excerpted from the full 70-line poem; the ellipses mark where lines have been skipped, not anything Tennyson wrote.
📝 WORD STUDY

Ulysses says experience is an “arch” through which he glimpses a world he has not yet traveled. Tap to check what he means.

⚡ TAP TO REVEAL
experience is an arch
Ulysses pictures everything he has already lived through as an archway. Standing under it, he can see past its far edge to a world he still has not visited, one that keeps receding the closer he gets. The more he experiences, the more he senses how much is still unknown.
🌱 WORD ROOTS
The word arch here comes from Latin arcus, “a bow or curved structure.” The same root appears in arc and archway. It is a different word from the “arch” meaning chief or mischievous, which comes from Greek archi-, “first, chief.”
⚡ WHAT THE LINES REVEAL

For each line or pair of lines, choose what it shows about Ulysses.

⚡ TRY IT · READ THE SPEAKER
What does each part of the speech reveal?
1. “By this still hearth, among these barren crags… That hoard, and sleep, and feed, and know not me.”
2. “How dull it is to pause, to make an end, To rust unburnish'd, not to shine in use!”
3. “Made weak by time and fate, but strong in will To strive, to seek, to find, and not to yield.”
💡 HOLD ONTO THIS POEM

Ulysses cannot stop wondering what lies beyond the edge of everything he has already seen. As you begin Out of the Silent Planet this week, watch for a very different kind of traveler: Ransom does not choose his journey the way Ulysses does. But by the novel's end, ask yourself whether Ransom comes to feel anything like what Ulysses feels here, a pull toward the unknown that will not let him go.

In your notebook or Organizer, jot one sentence: does Ulysses' hunger for the unknown sound admirable to you, or does it sound like restlessness that could hurt the people who depend on him? There is no single right answer.

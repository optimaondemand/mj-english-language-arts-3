The Plainest Speech in the Book

📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
The man who always told Emma the truth tells her one more.
When Mr. Knightley proposes, he does not perform. He says, “I cannot make speeches, Emma,” and then speaks anyway, plainly and truly. The same honesty that once stung Emma now reaches her as love. Read closely and the scene answers the question the whole module has asked: Emma can finally hear him because she has learned to see herself. Today you reread this scene and mine it for the lines your essay will use.
🎯 BY THE END OF THIS LESSON YOU WILL BE ABLE TO…
1
I will be able to explain what the proposal scene reveals about Emma’s growth.
2
I will be able to choose a line from the scene as evidence for the central question.

⚡ PART 2 · WHAT DOES THE SCENE REVEAL?

For each detail, choose what it shows about Emma’s growth or about the answer to the central question.

⚡ TRY IT · MINE THE SCENE
What does each detail reveal?
1. Emma now welcomes the same plain honesty from Mr. Knightley that once stung her.
2. “If I loved you less, I might be able to talk about it more.”
3. Before she can accept him, Emma reflects honestly on her own past errors.

💪 BEFORE YOU GO

The novel ends where its question began: with honesty. Emma can accept the truest words in the book only because she has learned to face the truth about herself. Memory work: drill all three passages; the proposal’s plainness echoes the honesty in “It was badly done, indeed!”

Lesson 2.3
When a Sentence Describes Its Object
Grammar Lesson
📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
Sometimes the action does not rename the object; it changes how the object is.
On Monday a second noun renamed the object: The crew named the world Malacandra. Today the word after the object is not a noun but an adjective, and it describes the result of the action: The strange light turned the rock gold. Here rock is the direct object, and gold is an adjective describing what the rock became. This pattern is subject, verb, direct object, object complement adjective. The test still works: ask what the object became. The rock became gold. Verbs like make, paint, turn, leave, find, and call often set up this pattern. It is a quick way to show change: an action and its effect on a thing, side by side.
🎯 BY THE END OF THIS LESSON YOU WILL BE ABLE TO…
1
I will be able to spot an adjective that describes the result of an action on the object.
2
I will be able to tell this pattern apart from one that renames the object with a noun.
📋 WHAT YOU NEED FOR THIS LESSON
▶
Your writing notebook and a pencil. About 5 to 10 minutes of focused practice.
📚 PART 1 · A WORKED EXAMPLE
⚡ QUICK CHECK-IN
In which sentence does the last word tell you what the object became? (No wrong answer.)

The test: find the direct object, then ask what the object became or was made to be. If the answer is a describing word, you have an object complement adjective.

“The thin air left Ransom dizzy.”
Subject: air. Verb: left. Direct object: Ransom. Object complement: dizzy, an adjective.
Test: what did the air make Ransom? Dizzy. “Dizzy” describes the result, so it is an object complement adjective.
⚡ PART 2 · YOUR TURN

Does the bold word rename the object (a noun) or describe what it became (an adjective)?

⚡ TRY IT · RENAME OR DESCRIBE?
Choose what the bold word is.
1. The pale sun turned the water blue.
2. The two men made Ransom their prisoner.
3. The long silence left the valley empty.
4. Ransom considered Devine a coward.
▶  In your notebook, write one sentence where an action makes the object a certain way (use make, turn, paint, or leave). Then click to see examples.
“The cold made his hands stiff.” Object: hands. Adjective: stiff.   “The long voyage left the crew weary.” Object: crew. Adjective: weary. Check: ask what the object became, and confirm the answer is a describing word.
💪 BEFORE YOU GO

A noun renames the object; an adjective describes what it became. Both patterns let you show change in a single sentence, which is exactly what a world-building writer needs. Memory work: keep learning the passage that names the three rational species of Malacandra, copied from your own copy.

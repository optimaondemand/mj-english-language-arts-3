Lesson 2.1
When a Sentence Renames Its Object
Grammar Lesson
📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
Some sentences give a thing a new name in the same breath.
You already know a sentence can have a subject, a verb, and a direct object: The crew elected a captain. The captain is the direct object; it receives the action. But watch what happens here: The crew elected Ransom captain. Now there are two words after the verb. Ransom is the direct object, and captain renames Ransom. That second noun is called an object complement, and when it is a noun that renames the direct object, the sentence follows this pattern: subject, verb, direct object, object complement noun. The test is simple. If the last noun renames the object (Ransom is the captain), it is an object complement. Writers reach for this pattern when one act changes what something is: a vote makes a person a leader, a choice makes a place a home.
🎯 BY THE END OF THIS LESSON YOU WILL BE ABLE TO…
1
I will be able to find the direct object and the noun that renames it.
2
I will be able to write a sentence that renames its object with a second noun.
📋 WHAT YOU NEED FOR THIS LESSON
▶
Your writing notebook and a pencil. About 5 to 10 minutes of focused practice.
📚 PART 1 · A WORKED EXAMPLE
⚡ QUICK CHECK-IN
In which sentence does the last word give something a new name? (No wrong answer.)

Here is the test. Find the direct object, then ask whether the next noun renames it. If you can say the object IS that noun, you have an object complement.

“The kidnappers called the planet Malacandra.”
Subject: kidnappers. Verb: called. Direct object: planet. Object complement: Malacandra renames the planet.
Test: the planet is Malacandra. The renaming holds, so “Malacandra” is an object complement noun.

▶  In your notebook, write one sentence that renames its object with a second noun (think of a vote, a name, or a title). Then click to see examples.
“The crew named the new world their home.” Direct object: world. Object complement: home.   “They elected her captain.” Direct object: her. Object complement: captain. Check that your last noun renames the object: the world is the home, she is the captain.
💪 BEFORE YOU GO

When a sentence renames its object, one act changes what something is. That is a useful tool for a story: naming a creature, declaring a place. Memory work: begin learning the passage that names the three rational species of Malacandra. Copy it carefully from your own copy.

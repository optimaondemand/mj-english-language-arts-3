Lesson 1.38
Its or It’s? Their or They’re?
Grammar Lesson
📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
One little apostrophe changes the meaning. Here is the test that never fails.
These pairs trip up almost every writer. Its (no apostrophe) shows possession: its means “belonging to it.” It’s (with apostrophe) is a contraction, short for it is or it has. The same goes for their (belonging to them) and they’re (short for they are). Here is the test: read the apostrophe word out as the full two words. If “it is” or “they are” makes sense in the sentence, use the apostrophe. If it does not, you want the plain possessive.
🎯 BY THE END OF THIS LESSON YOU WILL BE ABLE TO…
1
I will be able to choose between its and it’s using the “it is” test.
2
I will be able to choose between their and they’re using the “they are” test.
📋 WHAT YOU NEED FOR THIS LESSON
▶
Your writing notebook and a pencil. About 5–10 minutes of focused practice.
📚 PART 1 · A WORKED EXAMPLE
⚡ QUICK CHECK-IN
“___ a long way to Box Hill.” Read it as “it is a long way.” Does that make sense? (No wrong answer.)

Apply the test: swap in the full two words and listen.

“The carriage lost a wheel from its axle.” → “from it is axle” is nonsense, so use the possessive its.
“It’s a fine day for a walk.” → “It is a fine day” works, so use it’s.
“They’re expected at Hartfield by noon.” → “They are expected” works, so use they’re.
⚡ PART 2 · YOUR TURN

Use the “it is” or “they are” test, then pick the word that belongs in the blank.

⚡ TRY IT · APOSTROPHE OR NOT?
Which word fills the blank correctly?
1. Highbury has ___ own quiet charm.
2. ___ hard to say what Frank Churchill really wants.
3. The young people shared ___ plans for a ball.
4. ___ sure to call at Hartfield this afternoon.
▶  In your notebook, write one sentence using its and one using it’s. Then click to check your reasoning.
Example: “The town kept its old customs.” (belonging to the town) and “It’s clear that Emma has changed.” (it is clear). Read each apostrophe word as “it is” to confirm you chose right.
💪 BEFORE YOU GO

When in doubt, read the apostrophe word out as “it is” or “they are.” If it sounds wrong, drop the apostrophe. Use this test when you proofread your Quick Write this week. Memory work: drill all three of your passages.

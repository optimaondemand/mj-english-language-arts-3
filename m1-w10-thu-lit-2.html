Saying It Aloud: Recitation Rehearsal
Literature Lesson
📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
A memorized passage is yours for life. Today you rehearse saying it well.
You have carried three passages from Emma in your memory all module. This week you record one of them. A good recitation rests on three things. Accuracy: you say the words as Austen wrote them, in order, without skipping or adding. Audibility: you speak loudly and clearly enough that a listener catches every word. Pacing: you do not rush; you let the punctuation guide your pauses so the meaning comes through. Today you choose your passage, listen for your own weak spots, and rehearse.
🎯 BY THE END OF THIS LESSON YOU WILL BE ABLE TO…
1
I will be able to recite one Emma passage accurately from memory.
2
I will be able to speak it audibly and at a steady pace.
📋 WHAT YOU NEED FOR THIS LESSON
▶
A quiet space, the three passages below, and a device to record a short practice take.
📚 PART 1 · CHOOSE AND WARM UP
⚡ QUICK CHECK-IN
Which of the three passages do you know most surely by heart? (No wrong answer.)

Here are the three passages you have learned this module. You will record one of them. Read your chosen passage aloud once, slowly, before you do anything else.

Passage 1 · the opening:
“Emma Woodhouse, handsome, clever, and rich, with a comfortable home and happy disposition, seemed to unite some of the best blessings of existence; and had lived nearly twenty-one years in the world with very little to distress or vex her.”
Passage 2 · Mr. Knightley at Box Hill:
“It was badly done, indeed! You, whom she had known from an infant, whom she had seen grow up from a period when her notice was an honour, to have you now, in thoughtless spirits, and the pride of the moment, laugh at her, humble her.”
Passage 3 · Emma’s self-reckoning:
“With insufferable vanity had she believed herself in the secret of everybody’s feelings; with unpardonable arrogance proposed to arrange everybody’s destiny.”
⚡ PART 2 · WHAT MAKES A GOOD RECITATION?

For each note from a listener, choose which of the three qualities needs work.

⚡ TRY IT · ACCURACY, AUDIBILITY, OR PACING?
Which quality does each note point to?
1. “I lost the last few words; your voice dropped at the end.”
2. “You said it all, but it went by so fast I could not follow the meaning.”
3. “You said ‘pretty’ where the line reads ‘handsome.’”
▶  Record one practice take on your phone, listen back, and name your own weakest spot. Then click for a rehearsal tip.
Tip: mark the natural pauses in your passage with a small slash at each comma and period, then practice pausing there. Stand up, breathe before you begin, and speak to the back wall. Record once more before your real take.
➡️ WHAT YOU'LL DO NEXT

Final Recitation
Record yourself saying your chosen passage and submit it. (See the Recitation assignment page for how to record and submit.)
💪 BEFORE YOU GO

The words you can say from memory become part of how you think. Rehearse until your passage is steady, then record it with confidence. Memory work: this is the week your recitation is graded. Make your final take your best one.

📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
On Earth, one kind of creature does the thinking. Malacandra is different.
Malacandra is home to three intelligent races who share the planet as equals. The hrossa are makers of poetry and song, living by the water and hunting the dangerous hnakra. The seroni (or sorns) are tall thinkers, drawn to knowledge, history, and the study of the world. The pfifltriggi are builders and craftsmen who shape stone and metal with great skill. Each race is intelligent, but in its own way, and none rules the others. As you read Chapters 13 to 16 in your own copy, watch how each race shows a different gift of the mind. The question underneath it all: when Ransom first arrived, he assumed one of them must be the master. What did that assumption cost him?
🎯 BY THE END OF THIS LESSON YOU WILL BE ABLE TO…
1
I will be able to describe how each of the three races is intelligent in its own way.
2
I will be able to compare and contrast the three races using evidence from the reading.
📋 WHAT YOU NEED FOR THIS LESSON
▶
Your own copy of Out of the Silent Planet, Chapters 13 to 16, and your writing notebook.
📖 PART 1 · READ, THEN COMPARE
⚡ QUICK CHECK-IN
Think of three people you know who are each smart in a different way. Is there only one right kind of intelligence? (No wrong answer.)

Read Chapters 13 to 16 in your own copy. Keep a running note on each race: what they love, what they make, and how they treat the other two. When the text names a gift directly, you may copy one short attributed phrase into your notes.

📖 YOUR READING
Read Chapters 13 to 16 in your own copy of Out of the Silent Planet. This novel is under copyright, so the text is not reprinted here. Have your book open beside you.
⚡ PART 2 · WHICH RACE, WHICH GIFT?

For each description, choose the race it best fits.

⚡ TRY IT · MATCH THE GIFT
Which race does each line describe?
1. They make poetry and song and prize the great hunt.
2. They are the scholars, eager to understand and explain.
3. They are the makers, skilled in shaping stone and metal.
4. None of them rules the others; each honors the others’ gifts.
📝 PART 3 · WORD STUDY

The races of Malacandra are not all alike; they are distinct. Tap to check the meaning, then practice the word several ways in your Organizer.

⚡ TAP TO REVEAL
distinct
Distinct means clearly different and separate, easy to tell apart. The hrossa, seroni, and pfifltriggi are distinct: each has its own gift, its own work, and its own way of seeing the world.
🌱 WORD ROOTS
Distinct comes from Latin dis- (“apart”) and stinguere (“to prick or mark”). The same root shows up in distinguish (to mark the difference) and extinguish (to mark out, to put out).
📝 WORD STUDY · IN YOUR ORGANIZER
Write a sentence using distinct to describe two of the three races. Then rewrite it twice: once with a synonym, once expanding it (“distinct because…”).

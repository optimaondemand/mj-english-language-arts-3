👋 WELCOME
This year you will read six very different books and one poem — a novel of manners in an English village, a journey to another planet, a poem about wonder under the stars, a struggle for survival on an island, a small town's terrible tradition, and a trial that divides a Southern town — and in each one you will meet the same hard question in a new disguise: what does it cost to see clearly, and to act rightly on what you see?
You will read closely, build a strong vocabulary, sharpen your grammar and writing, and argue your own answers in essays, stories, and recitations. By the end of the year you will be a more careful reader and a more confident writer — and you will have thought hard about self-knowledge, empathy, conscience, and courage.

📝 HOW THIS COURSE WORKS
This is a self-paced course. You move through each week's lessons on your own, in Canvas. A facilitator grades your work, answers your questions by email and in the Canvas Inbox, and holds office hours — reach out any time you are stuck.
Every week follows the same rhythm: a Monday and Wednesday grammar lesson, a Tuesday and Thursday literature lesson, and a Friday Check-up discussion. Plus one Daily Practice (your handwritten writing notebook, turned in as photos) and one Quick Write (typed in Microsoft Word) each week. Each module ends with one major work — an essay, a story, or a project.

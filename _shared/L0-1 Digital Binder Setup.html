Lesson
0.1 · Digital Binder Setup
Time
~15 minutes
One time, at the start of the year
You'll need
Your school Microsoft 365 account
📁 WHY THIS MATTERS
A classical student keeps an orderly binder. This year your binder is digital: one tidy folder in OneDrive where every piece of ELA writing lives. Set it up once now and you will never scramble to find a file or wonder where to save your work. Everything in this course — Quick Writes, your reading journal, your module project — goes in the folder you build today.
📝 BUILD YOUR FOLDER — IN ORDER
1
Go to office.com and sign in with your school Microsoft account (not a personal one). New to signing in? See the M365 Setup & File Organization Guide linked in this module.
2
Open OneDrive. Click New → Folder and name it exactly Optima.
3
Open Optima. Make a folder inside it named ELA.
4
Open ELA. Make one more folder inside it named ELA8. This is your home for the whole course.
5
Check your path. When you open your folders in order, it should read:
OneDrive → Optima → ELA → ELA8
📁 HOW TO NAME YOUR FILES
Every file you save this year follows one pattern:
LastName_FirstName_AssignmentName_ELA8
Example: Smith_Jane_QuickWrite1_ELA8. Underscores between parts, and always end with _ELA8.
✅ YOU'RE DONE WHEN
☐
I can open Optima → ELA → ELA8 in my OneDrive.
☐
I know my file-naming pattern ends with _ELA8.
☐
I completed the short Digital Binder Setup check-in assignment in Canvas (a screenshot of my ELA8 folder, or a one-line confirmation).
💡 TIP
OneDrive saves your work automatically and keeps a history of past versions, so you are protected even if your computer closes unexpectedly. Save often anyway — it is a good habit.

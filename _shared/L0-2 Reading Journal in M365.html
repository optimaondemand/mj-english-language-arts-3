📖 WHAT A READING JOURNAL IS
Good readers think on the page. Your reading journal is the place you keep that thinking for each book we read: short notes on what you noticed, new vocabulary, and your answers to the weekly Notice · Name · Apply prompts. It is not graded for grammar — it is your workshop. Many of your best ideas for Quick Writes and the module project will start here.
This year your journal is a Microsoft Word document kept in your OneDrive ELA8 folder — one journal per module. (If you also like to jot notes by hand, you may, but your typed journal is the one you keep and sometimes submit.)
📝 SET UP YOUR MODULE 1 JOURNAL
1
Open Word from your school Microsoft account and create a new blank document.
2
Save it to OneDrive → Optima → ELA → ELA8 with the name: LastName_FirstName_ReadingJournal_M1_ELA8
3
At the top, use the Heading 1 style to title it Reading Journal — Module 1: Emma. Add your name and the date below it.
4
Each week, add a Heading 2 for that week (e.g., Week 1 — Volume I, Chapters 1-3) and write your notes underneath. You are building one growing document, not many tiny files.
📁 M365 Skill You're Practicing: Applying Heading Styles in Word (MOS Core). Headings let you build a clean, navigable document — a skill you will use in every course.
🎯 WHAT TO PUT IN EACH WEEK'S ENTRY
NoticeA line or moment that stood out as you read. Copy the quotation or note the page.
NameName what the author is doing — a strong verb, a symbol, a turning point, a vocabulary word.
ApplyA sentence or two of your own thinking, or a sentence you write imitating the author's move.
VocabularyTwo or three new words and what they mean in context.
💡 GOOD TO KNOW
Some assignments will ask you to submit your reading journal, or to paste a journal entry into a Quick Write. Because it already lives in your ELA8 folder and is named correctly, you will be ready — just open it and submit through the Canvas OneDrive tab. Start a fresh journal for each new module (change M1 to M2, and so on).

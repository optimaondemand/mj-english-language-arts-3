📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
A strong essay does not just argue; it answers the best objections first.
You have finished the novel. Now you turn nine weeks of reading into a plan. A good essay takes a clear position on the central question, then does something braver than most writers attempt: it names the strongest objections to that position and answers each one. An argument that has already met its toughest critics is far harder to knock down. Today you build that frame: your question, your position, three real objections, and your reply to each.
🎯 BY THE END OF THIS LESSON YOU WILL BE ABLE TO…
1
I will be able to state my position on the central question in one clear sentence.
2
I will be able to name three real objections to my position and sketch a reply to each.

📖 YOUR READING
Emma: full text (PDF), Vol. III, Ch. 13–end  [Teacher: confirm the Canvas file link on deploy.]
🎯 THE CENTRAL QUESTION
How does a person of strong feeling and good intent come to know herself well enough to act rightly toward others?
⚡ PART 2 · SORT THE PARTS OF AN OUTLINE

An outline has parts that do different jobs. Match each line below to its job.

⚡ TRY IT · POSITION, OBJECTION, OR REPLY?
What job does each line do in an essay outline?
1. “Emma comes to know herself only when someone she respects tells her the truth.”
2. “But Emma already feels ashamed at Box Hill before anyone scolds her, so maybe she corrects herself.”
3. “Her shame is real, but it is Mr. Knightley’s plain words that turn that feeling into a changed life.”
4. “One could argue it is heartbreak over Mr. Knightley, not honesty, that wakes Emma up.”
▶  In your Organizer, write your position in one sentence. Then click for a checklist of what makes a position strong.
A strong position is one sentence, takes a clear side, and could be argued against. “Emma changes” is too obvious to argue. “Emma changes only because a trusted friend tells her the truth” gives someone room to disagree, which is exactly what you want.
📝 PART 3 · WORD STUDY

Austen prizes “candour.” Tap to check the meaning, then practice the word several ways in your Organizer.

⚡ TAP TO REVEAL
candour
Candour means honesty and fairness in speaking, especially the willingness to say a hard truth kindly. It is the quality Mr. Knightley shows when he corrects Emma; it is what finally helps her see herself.
🌱 WORD ROOTS
You met this word’s root back in Week 8. See if you can recall it before reading on: candour comes from Latin candere, “to shine.” If it does not come back to you, revisit that week’s Word Roots note.
📝 WORD STUDY · IN YOUR ORGANIZER
Write a sentence using candour to describe a character. Then rewrite it twice: once with a synonym, once expanding it (“candour that…”). If candour belongs in your thesis, keep the best version for your essay.

💪 BEFORE YOU GO

The objections you raise are not weaknesses in your argument; they are its backbone. The essay that has already answered its critics is the one that holds. Memory work: drill all three passages so the right line is ready when your essay needs it.

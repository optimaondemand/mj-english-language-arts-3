Lesson 1.8
Words That Describe, Words That Point
Grammar Lesson
📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
Adjectives add color to a noun. Articles simply point to it.
An adjective describes a noun: it answers what kind? (a clever girl), which one? (that letter), or how many? (three sisters). The little words a, an, and the are a special kind of adjective called articles, and their only job is to point to a noun. Use a or an for any one of a kind (a proposal), and the for a particular one the reader already has in mind (the proposal Mr. Elton made). Austen chooses her describing words with great care, so learning to spot them helps you see exactly how she shapes a character.
🎯 BY THE END OF THIS LESSON YOU WILL BE ABLE TO…
1
I will be able to find adjectives and name the noun each one describes.
2
I will be able to choose between a/an and the.
📋 WHAT YOU NEED FOR THIS LESSON
▶
Your writing notebook and a pencil. About 5–10 minutes of focused practice.
📚 PART 1 · A WORKED EXAMPLE
⚡ QUICK CHECK-IN
“Hand me ___ book”: which word fits if you mean one you have not named yet? (No wrong answer.)

Look at a phrase from Highbury and name each describing word and what it points to:

“the pretty, agreeable young woman”
the → article (points to one particular woman)
pretty, agreeable, young → adjectives (each describes “woman”)
⚡ PART 2 · YOUR TURN
⚡ TRY IT · DESCRIBE OR POINT?
Choose the best answer.
1. In “a comfortable home,” the word comfortable is a…
2. In “the letter from Mr. Elton,” the word the is an…
3. Choose the article: “___ honest answer.”
▶  In your notebook, underline the three adjectives in this phrase, then click to check: “a small, neat, well-kept cottage.”
Adjectives: small, neat, well-kept (each describes “cottage”). “A” is the article.

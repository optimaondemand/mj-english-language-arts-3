📚 HOW TO PLAY

A sentence appears with one phrase bolded. Click the button that names what the bolded phrase is doing: a gerund (an -ing word acting as a noun), a participle (an -ing or -ed word acting as an adjective), an infinitive (to plus a verb), or none of these (a plain verb or a preposition plus a noun).

  1. You have 75 seconds on the clock.
  2. Each correct answer adds to your score, with a quick note on why.
  3. Each miss gives you a quick note on the right answer, then moves on.
  4. Answer as many as you can before time runs out, then see your final score.

💪 THE TEST TO REMEMBER

If you can swap the phrase for “something” or “it” and the sentence still works, it is a gerund. If the phrase describes a noun the way an adjective would, it is a participle. If it starts with “to” plus a verb, it is an infinitive. If none of those fit, it is just a verb doing its normal job, or a preposition plus a noun.

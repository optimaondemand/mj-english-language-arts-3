Lesson 1.46
Capital Letters That Do Real Work
Grammar Lesson
📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
A capital letter is a signal. It tells your reader, “this is a name, not just a word.”
Your final essay is due this week, so today you sharpen the small marks that make writing look finished. Capitalize the first word of a sentence. Capitalize a proper noun: the particular name of a person, place, or thing, such as Emma, Highbury, or Mr. Knightley. Capitalize the main words in a title: Emma, Pride and Prejudice; the small joining words like and, of, and the stay lowercase unless they begin the title. Do not capitalize a common noun just because it feels important: novel, marriage, friendship, and heroine stay lowercase. Getting this right is part of the clarity and correctness your essay rubric rewards.
🎯 BY THE END OF THIS LESSON YOU WILL BE ABLE TO…
1
I will be able to capitalize proper nouns and leave common nouns lowercase.
2
I will be able to capitalize the title of a book correctly inside my own sentence.
📋 WHAT YOU NEED FOR THIS LESSON
▶
Your essay draft (or your notes for it) and a pencil. About 5 to 10 minutes of focused practice.
📚 PART 1 · A WORKED EXAMPLE
⚡ QUICK CHECK-IN
Which one looks like it names one particular thing? (No wrong answer.)

Here is the test: if a word names one particular person, place, or thing, capitalize it. If it names a general kind of thing, leave it lowercase.

“Emma lives in Highbury.” → Emma and Highbury are particular names. Both capitalized.
“She wants to arrange a marriage.” → “marriage” is a general thing. Lowercase.
“Austen titled her novel Emma.” → “novel” is common; Emma is the title. Title capitalized and italicized.
⚡ PART 2 · YOUR TURN

In each sentence, one word is in question. Should that word be capitalized or stay lowercase?

⚡ TRY IT · CAPITAL OR LOWERCASE?
Choose the correct treatment for the word in question.
1. The word knightley in: “Mr. knightley speaks plainly.”
2. The word heroine in: “Emma is the heroine of the novel.”
3. The word although in: “although Emma means well, she misreads Harriet.”
4. The word and in the title: “Pride And Prejudice.”
▶  In your draft, find your thesis sentence and rewrite it here with every capital correct. Then click to see a corrected example.
Corrected example: “In Emma, Jane Austen shows that a clever heroine can still fail to know herself.” The title and the two names are capitalized; heroine stays lowercase. Your own thesis will differ. Check each capital against the test.
💪 BEFORE YOU GO

Run one capitalization pass over your draft today: names get capitals, general words stay lowercase, the title gets capitals and italics. Memory work: your recitation is graded this week. Say your chosen passage aloud once today and listen for the words you tend to drop.
